The Supreme Court of the United States is the only court mandated by the
Constitution and is the court of last resort. The Supreme Court bas original
‘yrisdiction overall disputes involving foreign ministers and interstate
disputes, and disputes between the federal and state governments. The
Court has jurisdiction of cases originating from any other court if the case
involves constitutional issues, federal laws or maritime treaties and laws.
The Supreme Court may affirm, modify, reverse or remand a decision of a
lower court. The Supreme Court is comprised of the Chief Justice and
eight associate justices, who are appointed by the President and confirmed
by the Senate. Among his or her responsibilities, each Justice is assigned
to serve as “Circuit Justice” for one or more of the United States Judicial
Circuits.
