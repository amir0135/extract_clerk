Alien Terrorist Removal Court

E. Barrett Prettyman U.S. Courthouse, 333 Constitution Avenue, NW,
Washington, DC 20001
Tel: (202) 354-3050 Fax: (202) 354-3023

Number of Judgeships: 5

The Alien Terrorist Removal Court was created by the Antiterrorism and
Effective Death Penalty Act of 1996, The court has the authority to
conduct all proceedings to determine whether an alien should be removed
from the United States on the grounds of being a terrorist. Applications for
removal proceedings are brought by the Attorney General of the United
States after being initiated and investigated by the Department of Justice.
Decisions made by this court can be appealed to the United States Court
of Appeals for the District of Columbia. The court is made up of five
district court judges ftom five judicial circuits. The judges are appointed
for five yeat terms by the Chief Justice of the United States. Judges may ‘
be redesignated by the Chief Justice.

The United States Tax Court hears disputes involving the Internal Revenue
Service and deficiencies or overpayment of taxes. The United States Tax
Court judges are appointed by the President and confirmed by the Senate
to 15-year terms. A chief judge is elected biennially by fellow judges.
Both recalled and special trial judges serve at the pleasure of the court.
Public trials are conducted by a single judge or a special trial judge. These
rulings can be appealed to the United States Courts of Appeals and.the. .
